# Lead scoring

callNest assigns each contact a 0–100 lead score so you can prioritise outreach. Higher is better.

## What feeds the score

Six signals, each with an adjustable weight in **Settings → Lead scoring**:

- Recency of the last call
- Total number of calls
- Average call duration
- Bookmark count
- Manual tags (configurable per-tag boost)
- Auto-tag rule matches

Disable any signal by sliding its weight to zero.

## Buckets

Scores fall into four colour-coded bands:

- **0–24** — Cold
- **25–49** — Warm
- **50–74** — Hot
- **75–100** — On fire

The badge appears on call rows, contact rows, and inside the floating bubble.

## Rule-driven boosts

Auto-tag rules can apply a one-time score boost when they match. Use this for "downloaded brochure" or "demo booked" style triggers — give them a high boost so the contact climbs your list.

## Tuning

Start with the defaults. After a week of real use, open the lead-scoring screen — the live histogram shows the spread of scores across your contacts. Drag weights until the buckets feel right.

Disable scoring entirely with the master toggle if you prefer raw chronology.

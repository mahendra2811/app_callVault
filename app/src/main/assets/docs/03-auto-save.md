# Auto-save inquiries

Auto-save catches numbers you keep talking to but never bothered to save. Once enabled, CallVault writes them to your phone's contact book automatically — using a name pattern you choose, in a contact group you control.

## How it picks numbers
A number qualifies when it has been involved in two or more calls in the last seven days, is not already in your contacts, and is not on the do-not-auto-save list.

## The name pattern
The default pattern is `callVault {number}` so the rows are easy to find later. You can change the prefix, suffix, and whether the SIM tag is appended. Examples:
- `callVault +91 98xxx 12345`
- `Lead-9876543210-SIM1`

## Group label
Auto-saved contacts go into a contact group you name (default: *CallVault Inquiries*). The group is created on first save. You can move contacts out of it any time — CallVault won't move them back.

## Disabling auto-save
Turn the master toggle off in **Settings → Auto-save**. Existing auto-saved contacts stay where they are; the app simply stops adding new ones.

## Privacy
Auto-save runs entirely on-device. No number ever leaves your phone.
